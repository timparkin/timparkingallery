obakesan said...

Tim

you have some nice scenery there, reminds me of the south west of Tassie (and Ive not been back home to Australia for too long now).

Perhaps its mentioned elsewhere, but I didn't see what you're scanning with (and at what dpi, yadda yadda). My personal experience is (like you've seemingly found) that Slide is more punchy straight up than Negative. Negative (by its nature) spreads a greater range of scene brightness into a narrower density range on the film. In low contrast situations especially this does not favour negative. Its often necessary to adjust it to get a contrast build up (which would have happened normally in the printing process). Personally I've found it easier to do that then try and tame down slides which look beautiful on the light box but I can just never duplicate them.

I guess that my next move will be to carry a mixture based on the expected light conditions and use the film most appropriate to the conditions :-) I am using a digital as my light meter these days my methdods are on my website (http://home.people.net.au/~cjeastwd/photography/film/digiExposure.html). Having a digital as a 5 million spot meter has the added side benefit of giving me a colour reference of my shot as well as documenting my settings (I'm often too lazy to write them down). However when it comes to colour matching them I find that I can't easilly get exactly the same reproduction between slide scan <> negative scan <> digital. So I end up just making what I find I like the look of :-)
